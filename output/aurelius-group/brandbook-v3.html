04 — Primary Logo

Primary Logo

Construction

The Aurelius Group logo consists of a heraldic shield containing the monogram "A" set in Cormorant Garamond Light, paired with the wordmark "AURELIUS" and the descriptor "GROUP". A single gold rule underscores the wordmark.

Usage

The primary logo should be used wherever possible. It is the preferred mark for all formal communications, corporate documents, and brand touchpoints. Always maintain the proportional relationship between shield and wordmark.

11 — Do's & Don'ts

Do's & Don'ts

Do

Use the logo at correct proportions with adequate clear space.

Don't

Do not stretch, skew, or distort the logo in any way.

Do

Use the reversed version on dark backgrounds.

12 — Tone of Voice

Tone of Voice

Voice Attributes

Authoritative

We speak with confidence born from expertise, never arrogance.

Measured

Every word is intentional. We favor precision over volume.

Warm

Professional yet approachable. We build relationships, not transactions.

Discreet

We suggest rather than shout. Understatement is our signature.

Writing Examples

Correct

"We partner with families to preserve and grow wealth across generations."

Incorrect

"We're the BEST investment firm and will make you RICH fast!!!"

Correct

"Your legacy deserves the same care that built it."

Incorrect

"Don't miss out on this incredible opportunity! Limited spots available!"

13 — Photography

Photography Style

Mood & Atmosphere

Photography should evoke timelessness, quiet luxury, and substance. Avoid trend-driven aesthetics.

Lighting

Natural light preferred. Warm tones, soft shadows. Golden hour quality.

Composition

Generous negative space. Rule of thirds. Shallow depth of field for portraiture.

Color Treatment

Desaturated warm palette. Muted golds, deep blues, warm grays.

Subject Guidelines

People

Candid moments over posed shots. Confidence without ostentation.

Spaces

Clean architecture, premium materials. Dubai skyline as context, not spectacle.

Details

Textured surfaces, fine craftsmanship. Close-ups that suggest rather than show.

Avoid

Stock photo cliches, ostentatious wealth displays, overly casual settings.
