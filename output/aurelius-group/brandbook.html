11 — Do's & Don'ts

13 — Photography

Photography Style

Mood & Atmosphere

Photography should evoke timelessness, quiet luxury, and substance. Avoid trend-driven aesthetics.

Lighting

Natural light preferred. Warm tones, soft shadows. Golden hour quality. Avoid harsh flash or clinical lighting.

Composition

Generous negative space. Rule of thirds. Shallow depth of field for portraiture. Architectural symmetry for spaces.

Color Treatment

Desaturated warm palette. Muted golds, deep blues, warm grays. No oversaturated colors or heavy filters.

Subject Guidelines

People

Candid moments over posed shots. Confidence without ostentation. Diverse, sophisticated professionals.

Spaces

Clean architecture, premium materials (marble, leather, dark wood). Dubai skyline as context, not spectacle.

Details

Textured surfaces, fine craftsmanship, quality materials. Close-ups that suggest rather than show.

Avoid

Stock photo cliches (handshakes, skylines), ostentatious wealth displays, overly casual settings.

15 — Presentations

Presentations & Web

Presentation Slides

All presentations follow a consistent template with these principles:

Title Slides

Navy background, centered logo with double frame, gold typography.

Content Slides

White or cream background. Left-aligned content. Logo watermark at 5% opacity bottom-right.

Data Slides

Charts use brand palette only: navy, gold, grays. No 3D effects. Minimal grid lines.

Closing Slides

Navy background with contact information and logo. Double frame optional.

Website Guidelines

The website should reflect the brand's premium positioning:

Layout

Generous whitespace. Max content width: 1200px. No sidebar clutter. Single-column hero sections.

Navigation

Minimal top nav in DM Sans. No mega-menus. Sticky header with logo, transparent on scroll.

Interactions

Subtle transitions (300ms ease). Fade-in on scroll. No parallax or aggressive animations.

CTAs

Gold outline buttons on light backgrounds. Cream outline on dark. DM Sans uppercase, 0.2em tracking.
